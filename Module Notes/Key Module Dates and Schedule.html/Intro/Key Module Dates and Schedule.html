Activity 1 - Linear Algebra & Probability Theory

Over this two-week period, the purpose of the activity is to help you: 

1. Become confident with the following Linear Algebra topics: 

  • Matrix addition 
  • Subtraction 
  • Multiplication 
  • Determining matrix determinants 
  • Inverse, cross-product, and Eigenvalues 
  • Developing code for these operations 

2. We will look at probability theory and use Python's Random module to create datasets for simulated random events. We will calculate some statistical properties of these datasets and compare these to our a priori estimates. Furthermore, we will plot the distributions of the data to provide further insights into the datasets that we have created. You will see similar data time and time again when dealing with real-world machine learning problems and it is important to understand the stochastic nature of data and machine learning algorithms.

3. Begin your understanding of what machine learning is, and further your understanding of machine learning through the description of a pertinent example of Photo OCR. We will also quickly have a look at reinforcement learning.

The code submission will be on the topics above.

The quiz will be on the topics in this Activity.

Activity 2 - Artificial Neurons

Over these two weeks, we will look at the simplest artificial neurons, also known as the perceptron. These are the building blocks of neural networks, and thus the fundamentals of deep learning. We will look at perceptron learning theory. We will apply this to two datasets, one that has a lot of overlap in the features, and another that is easily separable by a linear classifier. We will also look at scikit-learn’s implementation of a perceptron using gradient descent for training and compare it to the Perceptron learning rule implementation. You will further your skills in reading datasets with the Pandas library, and you will dive a bit more into the scikit-learn library. 

This activity will give you a grounding to understand the building block of neural networks, being artificial neurons/perceptrons, and concepts in machine learning such as weights, epochs, and learning rates. 

The code submission will be on the topics above.

Activity 3 - Logistics Regression and SVMs 

In this activity, we will turn our attention now to two very common and powerful machine learning algorithms, which build upon the perceptron and gradient descent algorithms you learned about previously. We will be investigating Logistic Regression and Support Vector Machines. SVMs you will find a lot in industry. Logistic Regression is also common, but perhaps you will find the discussion around the sigmoid activation function more in the context of RNNs, CNNs, etc. There are further activation functions that have been discussed in the literature, but once you understand the sigmoid activation function, it is easy to follow these other ones. 

The code submission will be on the topics above.
